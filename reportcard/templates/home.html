Literacy Assessment Using Android Technology

During a period offive weeks from the end of May to the end of June 2012 the Education team of the Port-à-Piment Watershed Millennium Village in Haiti piloted a literacy assessment method.In total, 50 children were assessed, 40 of whom were enrolled in traditional schools (either public or private) and 10 of whom were enrolled in a new, special program attached to a public school that caters to children who have not previously attended school.

The tests were created to follow the structure and objectives of literacy tests used by ASER, in India; they evaluate basic reading skills in a fast, easy-to-use way. These kinds of tests are also notable because they address four separate reading stages: letter recognition, word recognition, sentence comprehension, and story comprehension.

The unique aspect of this project was its use of technology. The results of the assessment were recorded on an Open Data Kit (ODK) data collection platform on Android-powered mobile phones. Using this technology allowed the results to be processed much more quickly than by traditional methods.
